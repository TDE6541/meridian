MEETING VIDEO EXCERPT — RESOLUTION 25-5420 MOTION AND AMENDMENT

Fort Worth City Council Day Meeting
Tuesday, September 30, 2025
Agenda Item: Resolutions, Item 1 — Resolution 25-5420
Subject: Setting the Regularly Scheduled City Council Work Sessions and City Council Meetings for January 2026 through December 2026

MOTION / AMENDMENT / VOTE SEQUENCE:

Councilman Chris Nettles moved to amend Resolution 25-5420 to keep the current twice-monthly public-comment slots and to add a public-comment period to each daytime meeting. Councilwoman Deborah Peoples seconded the amendment.

After approximately 40 minutes of debate and public testimony, the motion to amend failed.

During debate, Councilman Nettles stated: "I still cannot stomach to reduce the public comment."

Resident Bob Willoughby addressed the council during public comment, stating the proposed reduction "takes away free speech."

Following the failed amendment, the council voted on Resolution 25-5420 as presented by staff. Motion carried. Resolution adopted.

Context: City staff presented a proposed 2026 calendar that would schedule 10 standalone public-comment meetings, down from 15 scheduled in 2025. Staff noted some 2025 sessions were shortened or canceled because of quorum problems. The Nettles amendment would have restored the twice-monthly structure used in prior years. The amendment failed; the calendar was approved as recommended by staff.
